The Sport

Why Golf Captivates the World

Golf is more than a game — it is a conversation between the player and the landscape, between patience and precision. First played on the windswept linksland of Scotland in the 15th century, it has grown into one of the most popular recreational sports on Earth, enjoyed by over 66 million players worldwide.

Unlike most sports, golf is played on a course that is never exactly the same twice. Weather, pin placement, and the angle of the morning light conspire to make every round a fresh challenge — and a fresh opportunity for glory.

Whether you dream of walking Augusta National or simply breaking 90 at your local municipal course, the green is waiting for you.

The Majors & Beginner Tips

The Four Major Championships

  1. The Masters Tournament Augusta National GC, Georgia
    • Held every April
    • Only major played at the same course each year
    • Winner receives the iconic green jacket
  2. U.S. Open Championship Rotates among demanding U.S. venues
    • Known for brutally tough rough and narrow fairways
    • Organized by the USGA
  3. The Open Championship (The British Open) Links courses across the UK & Ireland
    • Oldest major — first held in 1860
    • Played on seaside links courses
    • Winner receives the Claret Jug
  4. PGA Championship Various U.S. venues
    • Organized by the PGA of America
    • Known for large, deep fields

Beginner Tips to Improve Your Game

  • Master the Grip
    1. Use a neutral grip — not too strong, not too weak
    2. The V's formed by thumb and forefinger should point to your right shoulder
    3. Maintain light, consistent pressure throughout the swing
  • Focus on the Short Game
    1. Spend 60 % of practice time on putting and chipping
    2. Learn to judge distance, not just direction
  • Understand Course Management
    1. Play to your strengths — avoid hero shots
    2. Aim for the center of the green, not the flag
    3. Know your distances for each club
  • Practice Consistently
    1. Range sessions 2–3 times per week beat one marathon session
    2. Always warm up with short irons before long clubs
